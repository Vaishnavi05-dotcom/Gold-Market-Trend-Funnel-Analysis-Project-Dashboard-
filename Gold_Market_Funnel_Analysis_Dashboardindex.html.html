Market Commentary
◆Gold acts as a proven hedge against inflation — the 2020 price surge (+43.1%) coincided with pandemic-driven economic uncertainty (RBI data).
◆Prices rose during periods of elevated CPI in India (2022–23), reinforcing gold's role as an inflation-protection asset.
◆The 6-year CAGR of 15.4% significantly outperforms the 10-year average FD rate (~6.5%) and Nifty 50 average annual return (~12%).
◆Slower growth in 2021 (–0.6%) suggests temporary market stabilisation post the 2020 pandemic-driven rally.
◆The 2024 record high of ₹79,000/10g driven by global geopolitical tensions, Fed rate cut expectations, and strong central bank buying (WGC Q3 2024).

Conclusions and Recommendations
01Gold prices demonstrate a robust upward trend (CAGR 15.4%, 2019–2024), driven by inflation hedging, global uncertainty, and strong central bank purchases — data verified via MCX India and WGC.
02Digital gold and ETFs are reshaping the investment landscape, now capturing 56% combined market share — brands should invest in digital-first customer acquisition strategies.
03The largest investment funnel drop-off occurs at "Awareness → Interest" (–42%), indicating need for targeted financial literacy campaigns and simplified onboarding flows.
04Jewelry purchase conversion falls sharply above ₹65,000/10g price threshold — retailers should consider EMI schemes, lightweight collections, and festive season promotional pricing.
05The 2024 price record (₹79,000/10g) is underpinned by structural drivers — geopolitical risk premium, Fed pivot expectations, and RBI gold reserve accumulation — suggesting sustained elevated prices.
06Age group 31–45 represents the highest investor density and should be the primary target for SGB and ETF cross-sell campaigns, given risk-return awareness in this cohort.
